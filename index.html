The Gameplay and Stakes at Hand.

Dead by Daylight is an asymetrical and multiplayer horror game where the goal is to escape and survive.

You are faced against 1 killer and must survive with 3 other people and work together to escape from them.

(At least that's what the game wants but you could also be a lone survivor till the end of the match).

As survivor, you must fix 5 out of 7 generators placed on the map to power 2 exit gates.

If only one survivor remains, a hatch can spawn on the map that is placed as a 3rd escape option

As killer, you must hook all 4 survivors 3 times or kill them before they can escape.

Mouse and keyboard is the most optimal way to play this game and it is also crossplay between different

platforms. The biggest difference in gameplay between controller and mouse is the camera movement

speed is significantly faster on mouse and keyboard. This matters heavily for playing killer or

survivor as that allows you to see your surroundings which brings me to the next important gameplay loop.

Survivor Gameplay

Part of the gameplay involves dodging and "looping" each killer while playing as survivor. Looping in this

case means to have the killer chase you for as long as possible. Each second you take their attention is

a moment that allows your teammates to fix a generator, which makes it count. The other reason you need to

dodge is due to a 3 health state system. When hit the first time, you are just injured until you can be healed

by yourself via medkit(image below) or a teammate.

Narrative Arc and Lore

So what is Dead by Daylight? Each single time you play, you are playing in a "realm" or like a plane of existence.

You can also think of it as you playing in a really violent chess board as pawns of a being called "The Entity".

Each character is stolen from another plane of existence similar to earth and is brought here to survive trails

against monsters, killers, and evil forces from different films and intellectual properties.

Many characters were taken by force to play by the entity's game. Some killers however took interest and

barged their way into the realms to ensure carnage and chaos.

How does this relate to our readings?

There is actually quite a few of the readings from the discussion post that can be applied here.

For the reasoning of why we play, this game exemplies the thrill of a back and forth battle.

It is a constant battle to keep pressure throughout the game. Given that there is a power

dynamic as there is only 1 killer vs 4 people, the killer's task is to kill everyone as

quickly as possible. By the end of the game, we feel the end of the repition that Frued

had talked about. When you first start out the game, it tends to be really scary.

However, when you start to accumulate many hours of gameplay, it becomes more like

an action movie loop gameplay where you know what to expect and the fear factor goes away.

Conclusion

Dbd as the term is coined by many is a game that is very close to my heart. It is one that I have played

I also want to pursue horror one day, which is why this game also motivates me to make something new in the genre.

The other part that makes the loop fun is that you get different teammates every single time.

While you may get the same killer with a similar build each time, everyone plays different from one another.
